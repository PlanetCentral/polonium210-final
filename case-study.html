A real-world example of Polonium-210's lethal potential

The Litvinenko Case

In 2006, Alexander Litvinenko, a former Russian spy, fell victim to a carefully orchestrated assassination involving Polonium-210. He was poisoned by a lethal dose hidden in a cup of tea during a meeting in London, marking the first known case of deliberate radioactive poisoning.

The investigation into Litvinenko’s death revealed traces of Polonium-210 on multiple objects, including airplanes, hotels, and restaurants. The case highlighted the ease with which radioactive substances can be weaponized and raised global concerns about the misuse of nuclear materials.

Litvinenko’s agonizing death underscored the devastating effects of Polonium-210 on human health. His case remains a stark reminder of the importance of stringent security measures to prevent the illegal acquisition and misuse of radioactive elements.
